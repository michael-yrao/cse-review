## The Two Paths

### Path A: Staff Engineer (IC Track)

Best fit if you want deep technical work, not managing people.

**What "Staff" means at big tech**: You define technical direction across multiple teams, your decisions have multi-quarter impact, you mentor senior engineers, and you drive org-level technical strategy — not just your squad.

**Timeline: 3–5 years**

| Phase | Window | Focus |
|-------|--------|-------|
| 1. Close the interview gap | Now – Dec 2026 | Complete Blind 75 / NeetCode 150. Hit at least one FAANG screen. Interview at ≥3 big-tech adjacent companies (Stripe, Coinbase, Bloomberg, Robinhood, Jane Street, Two Sigma) — these value fintech depth and are more reachable than FAANG cold |
| 2. Land L5 (or equivalent) at a top-tier company | Jan–Jun 2027 | Target companies where your GL/reconciliation/fintech domain gives you an edge. Stripe, Robinhood, and trading firms all hire for this. Expect L5 (Senior equiv) first — don't hold out for L6 on day 1 |
| 3. Build Staff-level scope | 2027–2029 | Own a technical domain, not a feature. Propose and drive cross-team initiatives. Produce at least one design doc that outlasts your tenure. Mentor 2–3 engineers. Get your skip-level to know your name |
| 4. Promote to Staff | 2029–2031 | This is a real promotion with a bar. Most companies want: (a) cross-team technical influence, (b) a flagship project they can point to, (c) strong peer/skip endorsements |

**Public signal to build in parallel** (any one of these is enough):
- Write 3–5 technical posts on fintech system design (reconciliation, audit trails, multi-jurisdiction compliance)
- Contribute to an open-source fintech / financial data project
- Speak at a meetup or conference once a year

### Path B: CTO (Leadership Track)

Best fit if you want to build a company or run engineering at scale.

**Realistic CTO flavors**:
| Type | Realistic Timeline | Notes |
|------|--------------------|-------|
| CTO of your own startup | 1–3 years | You already did Progressive Overflow — do it again, bigger, with funding |
| CTO of an early-stage startup (Series A–B) | 2–4 years | Move to a 20–100 person company as VP/Head of Engineering, earn trust, get promoted to CTO |
| CTO of a big tech company | Not realistic in <10 years | Big tech CTOs are typically former VPs/SVPs with 20+ YOE. This is not the right target at 32 |

**If startup CTO is the goal**:
1. Start taking on 1–2 direct reports in your current role to build management muscle
2. Network in the fintech startup ecosystem (NYC has a deep one — Capital One, Brex, Ramp, Pipe, Mercury all have alumni who found companies)
3. By 34–36, move to a Series A fintech as VP Engineering or Head of Engineering
4. By 36–40, either get promoted to CTO or co-found your own company

## What 32 Actually Means

32 is not late. Most Staff Engineers hit that title at 30–35. The engineers who feel "behind" at 32 are usually benchmarking against outliers (the 28-year-old Staff at Google) rather than the median. Your fintech depth is worth more than another year of generic CRUD experience at a startup. The real risk isn't your age — it's staying Senior at the same company indefinitely without forcing a calibration. Moving companies (or interviewing seriously) is the fastest way to find out where the market prices you.
